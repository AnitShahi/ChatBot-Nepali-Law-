Understanding Nepali Law

Nepali law is a diverse and comprehensive legal system that draws from various influences, including Hindu legal traditions, British common law, and modern democratic principles. The Constitution of Nepal, adopted in 2015, is the supreme law of the land and outlines the fundamental rights and duties of citizens.

Key Areas of Nepali Law

  • Civil Law: Governs personal and property rights, including family law, inheritance, and contracts.
  • Criminal Law: Defines crimes and prescribes punishments for offenses such as theft, assault, and fraud.
  • Property Law: Regulates the ownership, transfer, and use of land and property, a critical area in a predominantly agrarian society.
  • Constitutional Law: Focuses on the interpretation and application of the Constitution, including the protection of fundamental rights.
  • Environmental Law: A growing field that addresses the protection of natural resources and the environment.
